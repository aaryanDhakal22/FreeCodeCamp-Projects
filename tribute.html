Gilmour's "The Black Strat"

David Gilmour performs live on stage at Madison Square Garden on April 12, 2016 in New York City.

"A guitar that is scuffed,
chipped and bearing the marks

and drill to it but all that adds to its value."

History:

Gilmour purchased the guitar, a 1969 model with a maple cap fingerboard and large headstock, in 1970 from Manny's Music in New York City to replace a similar guitar his parents bought him for his 21st birthday, which had been lost while touring with Pink Floyd in the United States in 1968. The Black Strat was originally a sunburst colour, but had been repainted black at Manny's. Since then, it has undergone numerous modifications.

Initially it had the following specs:

  • Alder body
  • Rosewood neck with large headstock
  • Sunburst finish
  • Fender late 60's single-coil pickups
  • 3-ply white pickguard
  • Fender tuning machine
  • Fender synchronized tremolo bridge with normal size arm.
  • 3-way selector switch

Modifications done to the guitar:

  • 1983 reissue Maple neck with normak headstock
  • Black paintjob
  • Fender 1971 reissue pickups in neck and middle and Seymour Duncan custom wound SSL-1C bridge pickup
  • 1-ply black pickguiard
  • Gotho vintage tuning machine
  • Custom mini-toggle switch on the pickguard for neck/bridge combo
  • 5-way selector switch
  • Fender synchronized tremolo bridge with short size arm.

Sale:

In early 2019 Gilmour announced he would auction many of his guitars for charity, including the Black Strat and the #0001 Strat. On June 20, 2019, the Black Strat sold for a price of US$3,975,000, becoming the most expensive guitar ever sold at that time. The guitar was purchased by guitar collector and Indianapolis Colts owner Jim Irsay.
